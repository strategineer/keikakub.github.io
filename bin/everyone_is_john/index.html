  1. The GM reveals John's tagline to players.
  2. The PCs talk through and create their Voices' skills (GM must approve all skills), adding them to the spreadsheet. - Choose two specific skills, gain 10 Willpower - Choose three, gain 7 Willpower - For maximum fun, don't pick skills that are similar to skills that other players are doing
  3. The PCs secretly writes down their Level 1, 2 and 3 Obsessions (Each PC must secretly send their obsessions to the GM for approval, some private back-and-forth may occur to make them just right). - Obsessions are the insane (and sometimes nonsensical) drives of each Voice. Completing them rewards points. - Each level of obsession should be progressively harder to achieve. - Each should be relatively simple and written in a short sentence.
  4. Play begins.

How to Play

Willpower

  • Each Voice starts with 10 (or 7 if they've chosen three skills) willpower
  • Voices spend willpower in two ways:
    • In Tests for Control, to assert their dominance and take control of John.
    • To improve the chances of their Skill Checks succeeding

Skill Checks

  • Roll a six-sided die:
    • Succeed on a 3 or better for skills you have
    • Succeed on a 6 for skills you don't have
  • Before the roll, you can spend Willpower to add a +1 to the result

Tests for Control

Tests for control occur when:

  • John wakes up. (Yes… this also means at the start of the session.)
  • John is injured.
  • Any Obsession is completed.
  • Any Check is failed.
  • John Gets Distracted and Falls Asleep (pg. 19).

Each Voice sends a private message to the GM with their bid (in Willpower points)

The Voice with the highest bid takes control of John. If multiple Voices tie for the highest bid, then they roll off to see who becomes the active Voice.

The Voice who becomes active loses the amount of Willpower they bid. All others keep their bids.
