Light and refreshing. Feels great to sip outside on a hot summer day.
Inspired by: The Joy of Mixology by Gary Reagan (p. 252)

Ingredients

  • 4 lime wedges
  • 2 to 3 teaspoons of granulated sugar
  • 8 to 10 fresh mint leaves
  • 2 oz light rum
  • Club soda
  • 2 or 3 mint sprigs for garnish

Steps

  1. Muddle the lime wedges, sugar, and mint leaves in a mixing glass until the sugar is completely dissolved, all the juice is extracted from the limes, and the mint is thoroughly integrated into the juice.
  2. Add ice and the rum to the mixing glass
  3. Shake and strain into a collins glass filled with crushed ice.
  4. Top with club soda, and add the garnish.

Legend

Muddle
Put the ingredients into an empty glass and grind them with a muddler until all the juices have been extracted from the fruit and any sugar in the recipe is completely dissolved.
Shake and Strain
Fill a shaker two-thirds full of ice and add the ingredients in the order given. Shake for approximately 15s and strain the drink into the appropriate glass.
