Homemade Ginger Beer

  • homebrew
My homebrewed ginger beer recipe. It's really good.
Inspired by: https://www.reddit.com/r/Homebrewing/comments/p3fudz/basic_ginger_beer_recipe_for_anyone_curious/

Ingredients

  • 1 gallon water
  • 3 thumb-sized piece organic ginger
  • 2 cups sugar (preferably brown)
  • 1/2 tbsp citric acid (or wine maker’s acid blend or lime or lemon juice)
  • 1 tsp yeast nutrient (optional but recommended)
  • 1/8 tsp yeast

Steps

  1. Boil water.
  2. While water heats up, peel and slice the ginger into 1/8-inch thick pieces. Push down on the pieces with your knife to soften the ginger. Place into cheesecloth/hop bag.
  3. When water hits boil, stir in sugar, then add ginger. Swirl the bag to help steep the ginger.
  4. Boil for 30 minutes.
  5. Cool, add to jug (ideally a jug with an airlock but in a pinch you can use a balloon with a tiny hole in it as a cap), add acid blend and yeast nutrient, pitch yeast.
  6. Wait for fermentation to end (should take about 3-4 days).
  7. Pour ginger beer from the jug into swing top bottles (like you would use for kombucha) with a funnel.
  8. Midway through pouring the ginger beer into the bottles, add 2 oz of simple sugar (pre-dissolved sugar basically) or honey into each bottle.
  9. Store in refrigerator for up to a week (maybe longer? I have no clue).
  10. Let the ginger beer settle for a few hours before drinking especially for bottles with a lot of sediment.

Notes

NB: DO NOT SHAKE THE BOTTLES, the yeast/sediment produced by the fermentation process tastes nasty.
