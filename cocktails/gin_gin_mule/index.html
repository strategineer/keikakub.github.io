Gin-Gin Mule

  • ⚖️
  • gin
  • mint
Tastes like a less minty and more gingery Southside cocktail. It's dayum good.
Inspired by: The Joy of Mixology by Gary Reagan (p. 213)

Ingredients

  • 3/4 oz fresh lime juice
  • 1 oz simple syrup
  • 2 mint sprigs (one for muddling and one for garnish)
  • 1 3/4 oz Tanqueray gin
  • 1 oz homemade ginger beer
  • 1 lime wheel, for garnish

Steps

  1. Muddle lime juice, simple syrup, and a mint sprig in a mixing glass.
  2. Add the gin, ginger beer, and ice.
  3. Shake and strain into an ice-filled highball glass, add garnishes and straws.

Legend

Muddle
Put the ingredients into an empty glass and grind them with a muddler until all the juices have been extracted from the fruit and any sugar in the recipe is completely dissolved.
Shake and Strain
Fill a shaker two-thirds full of ice and add the ingredients in the order given. Shake for approximately 15s and strain the drink into the appropriate glass.

Notes

  • Recipe for homemade ginger beer
  • This cocktail specifically tastes best with Tanquery gin, although feel free to use whatever you have.
